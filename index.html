Product Pricing

  • In GI GLM's are used to price products
  • The process can be involved and subjective
  • We can use machine learning techniques to:

    • Identify important features

    • Systematically validate and rank models

  • This speeds up the modelling process
  • Allows cheap and easy experimentation
  • External data sources can be evaluated easily

Underwriting

  • Underwriting is the process of evaluating risk
  • Needs skill & judgement - takes time to acquire
  • The process has:

    • Multiple conflicts of interest

    • Mistrust and dishonesty are built-in

    • Lots & lots of quantitative & qualitative data

  • Underwriting is the poster child for Data Analytics
  • Underwriting jobs will not exist in 10 years*

*my personal opinion

Fraud

Fraud can happen at a lot of stages

  • At application
  • During underwriting
  • When a claim is made or during a claim
  • Machine Learning is great at spotting:

    • Unusual features

    • and/or connections in:

      • High Dimensional Data

      • Large Amounts of unstructured data

Capital Management

  • Solvency II implemented in January 2016
  • Model based approach - lots of parameters to estimate
  • The more information you can extract from your data:
    • The more appropriate your risk parameters will be
    • A better understanding of risk can reduce its volatility
    • In turn this can decrease capital requirements
  • A small reduction in volatility can have a big effect on capital requirements

Regulation

  • Regulation is a huge cost for financial companies
  • Breaches have a financial & reputational cost
  • We can use machine learning techniques to identify:
    • Anomalies in data that suggest things are going wrong
    • Highlight increased operational risk
  • Machine learning can be used to interpret:
    • Reams of legislation and rules
    • Aide compliance with regulations
  • CBI & FCA looking at using data analysis and ML
